1.   OVERVIEW

1.1  Product Summary
ARB ResearchHub is a web platform built for the Faculty of Engineering at the University of Lagos. Its purpose is to digitise the research project submission process and to make approved research available as a publicly searchable library. Registered students upload their completed projects, an administrator reviews and publishes them, and the approved papers become accessible to anyone through a search-driven research hub.
1.2  Context
Engineering students at UNILAG produce research projects that are currently submitted and reviewed manually, and once completed are not collected anywhere the wider community can find them. ARB ResearchHub automates the submission and review process and builds an openly accessible library of approved work.
This version deliberately narrows the scope of the original PRD. The supervisor role and the two-stage defense workflow are removed; the administrator performs the review. The library, previously behind authentication, is now public. This keeps the build focused while opening the most valuable output Ś the body of approved research Ś to the public.
1.3  Objectives
Provide registered students with a simple, digital flow for uploading completed research projects.
Give administrators a clear dashboard to review submitted projects and decide on them.
Allow administrators to approve and publish projects, making them accessible to the public-facing library.
Allow members of the public to come into the platform and search the research hub for resources.
Provide excellent search: a keyword query returns every published paper whose content contains that term, alongside an AI-powered search that understands natural-language queries against the hub.

2.   SCOPE

2.1  In Scope (v2.0)
Authentication and role-based access (Student, Admin).
Student project submission: upload of a completed project document and its metadata.
Admin review workflow with approve / request revision / reject decisions.
Admin publishing: approved projects are published to the public library; the Admin can unpublish.
Public research library: browse and search approved papers without an account.
Keyword search across paper metadata and full document text, with relevance ranking and highlighted results.
AI-powered (semantic) search for natural-language querying of the library.
Admin dashboard and basic platform statistics.
In-app notifications to students on review outcomes.
2.2  Out of Scope (v2.0)
Supervisor role and supervisor dashboards. Review is performed by the Admin.
The two-stage / dual-defense workflow. Replaced by a single project submission.
Supervisorľstudent assignment, including CSV or XLSX bulk assignment.
Native mobile applications. The platform is web-first (responsive).
Public user accounts. The public side is search-and-read only; no comments, ratings, or logins for visitors.

4.   FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

4.1  Authentication & Onboarding
4.1.1  Registration
A student registers with their UNILAG email address (@unilag.edu.ng). The system validates the email domain; non-UNILAG addresses are rejected.
During registration the student provides their name, department, and matric number.
On successful registration, a verification link is sent to the provided email. The account is inactive until the link is clicked.
Admin accounts are not self-registerable; they are seeded at launch or created by an existing Admin.
4.1.2  Login & Sessions
Students and Admins log in with their email and password. Sessions are managed with JWT tokens (access and refresh).
Users can request a password reset by entering their email; a time-limited reset link is sent.
The public library requires no authentication. Only uploading and administration sit behind login.
4.2  Project Submission
A submission is a single record. It is created once by a student and covers one complete research project.
4.2.1  Submission Record
Each submission contains the following fields:
Title of the research project.
Abstract.
Author name and matric number (auto-populated from the student account).
Department and academic year / session.
Tags / keywords (optional).
Project document Ś the full paper as a single PDF.
Submission status (see Section 4.2.3).
4.2.2  Submission Rules
A student may have only one active submission at a time.
A student can resubmit revised materials in response to a &apos;Revision Requested&apos; decision, replacing the previous upload.
A student cannot edit a paper once it is published. Changes after publication require Admin action.
4.2.3  Submission Status Model
Status

4.3  Admin Review
4.3.1  Review Dashboard
The Admin dashboard shows all submissions grouped by status, with submissions pending review surfaced first and sorted by submission date.
The Admin can open a submission, read its metadata, and view or download the uploaded document.
4.3.2  Review Actions
Approve Ś approves and publishes the project to the public library. The document is indexed for search automatically.
Request Revision Ś returns the submission to the student with written comments. Comments are required.
Reject Ś closes the submission. It does not enter the library. Comments are optional but encouraged.
Review decisions and comments are stored and visible to the student.
4.3.3  Publishing
When the Admin approves a submission, the paper is automatically published to the library. No separate step is required.
The Admin can unpublish a paper at any time, removing it from the library without deleting the submission record, and can re-publish it later.
The Admin can edit a published paper&apos;s metadata and tags.
4.4  Research Library & Search
4.4.1  Access
The library is fully public. No login is required to search, browse, read, or download a published paper.
Only papers with status &apos;Published&apos; appear in the library. Drafts, pending, rejected, and unpublished records are never visible to the public.
4.4.2  Browse & Filter
Visitors can browse library entries and filter by department, academic year / session, and tags.
Each library entry displays: title, author, department, year, abstract, and tags.
Visitors can download the full paper PDF from the detail view.
4.4.3  Keyword Search
Search runs against paper titles, abstracts, and the full text of each document, so that any word appearing anywhere in a paper makes that paper findable.
When a paper is published, the system extracts the full text of the uploaded document and stores it as searchable content.
A keyword query is matched against the title, abstract, tags, author, and full document text.
Results are ranked by relevance, with matches in the title and abstract weighted above matches in the body.
Each result displays a highlighted excerpt showing where the search term appears.
Search tolerates partial words, word stems, and minor spelling errors, and supports multi-word queries.
4.4.4  AI-Powered Search
In addition to keyword search, the library provides an AI-powered search that understands the meaning of a query rather than matching exact words. A visitor can describe what they are looking for in plain language and receive the most relevant papers, even where the wording differs from the text of those papers.
When a paper is published, the system generates a vector representation (embedding) of its content.
A natural-language query is matched against these representations by similarity, returning papers that are conceptually related to the query.
Keyword and AI-powered results are combined so that exact-word matches and meaning-based matches both contribute to the ranking.
4.4.5  Implementation Notes
Keyword search uses PostgreSQL full-text search (a tsvector index over title, abstract, tags, and document text) with relevance ranking, highlighted excerpts, and trigram matching for spelling tolerance.
AI-powered search uses vector embeddings stored in the same PostgreSQL database (pgvector), so no separate search service is required at this scale.
4.5  Admin Dashboard
Review queue Ś submissions awaiting action, with counts by status.
Published-paper management Ś find any published paper to unpublish, re-publish, or edit its metadata and tags.
Platform statistics Ś total students, submissions by status, and published-paper count.
4.6  Notifications
Notifications are delivered in-app. Email notifications are a desirable enhancement but not a v2.0 requirement.
A bell icon in the navigation bar shows the count of unread notifications.

6.   NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

Area
Requirement
Security
Student and Admin routes are protected by JWT-based authentication. Role-based access control is enforced server-side on every API endpoint. Passwords are hashed (bcrypt or equivalent). HTTPS is enforced in all environments.
Public Access
Library search, browse, and read endpoints require no authentication and serve only published content. These endpoints are rate-limited to prevent abuse.
File Storage
Uploaded PDFs are stored in a cloud object store (e.g. Supabase Storage or AWS S3). Files are not served directly from the application server. Published papers are served via a CDN.
Search Performance
Keyword and AI-powered search responses should return within 2 seconds under normal load. Text extraction, indexing, and embedding generation run in the background when a paper is published, so the Admin action stays responsive.
Performance
Library browse and detail pages should load within 2 seconds under normal load. File uploads display a progress indicator; the UI does not block during upload.
Scalability
The backend is stateless and horizontally scalable. Database connections are pooled. File storage is external and unbounded by server disk.
Reliability
The system targets 99% uptime in production. Database backups are taken daily.
Accessibility
The frontend meets WCAG 2.1 AA contrast requirements. All interactive elements are keyboard-navigable and have visible focus states.
Browser Support
The platform supports the current and previous major versions of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge.

8.   OPEN ITEMS & ASSUMPTIONS

8.1  Open Items
Author identity Ś how much author information (name, matric number, department) is shown on the public page versus kept internal should be decided before the library detail page is built.
Rejected submissions Ś the current flow ends with a rejection. A formal process for what happens next (a new submission, or an appeal to the Admin) should be defined.
Email notifications Ś in-app only for v2.0. The decision on whether to add transactional email should be made before the notification module is built.
Co-authored projects Ś whether a project may list multiple authors, and how that maps to the single submitting student account, should be confirmed.
8.2  Assumptions
All students have a valid @unilag.edu.ng email address. Students without one cannot self-register and are out of scope for this version.
Each project is submitted as a single PDF, and a student has at most one active submission at a time.
Admin approval is the only gate before a paper is published. No additional approval step is required.
The public library is search-and-read only. Visitors cannot comment, rate, or upload.
The corpus is at faculty scale, so PostgreSQL handles both keyword and AI-powered search without a dedicated search engine.
